THE WORLD BANK ORGANIZATION

THE Study OF THE WORLD BANK ORGANIZATION. Every year, the world produces 2.01 billion tonnes of municipal solid garbage, with at least 33% of that — to put it conservatively — not being managed in an environmentally sound manner. The average amount of garbage created per person each day is 0.74 kilos, however, it varies greatly, ranging from 0.11 to 4.54 kilograms. Looking to the future, worldwide garbage is anticipated to reach 3.40 billion tonnes by 2050, more than doubling population growth over that time.

ABOUT MODEL

To detect the objects, we used a pre-trained image detection models: DenseNet-121. DenseNet-121 was pre-trained on ImageNet and can distinguish 1000 classes of objects. We have trained the dataset using VGG16 Transfer Learning technique of CNN for classification. The model is fined tuned to distinguish the image in 6 categories- cardboard, glass, metal, paper, plastic, trash. The results of the classification also lets the user know if the item should be recycled, composted, put into the general trash or needs special treatment (hazardous materials). User also get the information on much CO2 emission they avoided.
